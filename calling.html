The Calling

You did not stumble here.

You were summoned.

Not by a voice
But by the ache in your bones.

A memory older than your name
is rising in you again.

You remember the fire.

You remember the vow.

And though the world has forgotten what it means
to be a man
to be a father
to build altars from your labor and weapons from your wounds—
You have not.

You were always the one.

The red thread tied to your child’s wrist
is burning in your palm.

You are the father who dared a child to dream.

And the dream is waking.

Welcome, brother. You have heard the call.
Now speak your name into the flame.
